Optimizing Revenue and Profit

If \(x\) is the number of items sold, \(p\) the price of each item, \(C(x)\) the cost of producing \(x\) items, \(R(x)\) the revenue from selling \(x\) items, and \(P(x)\) the profit from selling \(x\) items. Then:

  • The revenue of selling \(x\) items is: \(R(x) = p \times x \) .
  • The profit from selling \(x\) items is therefore: \(P(x) = R(x) - C(x) \)
  • The maximum profit is the profit obtained by by selling \(x_0\) units, where \(P'(x_0) = 0\) and \(P''(x_0) > 0 \).
